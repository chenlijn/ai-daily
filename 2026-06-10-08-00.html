Method: Role-Agent uses one LLM in dual roles, as both acting agent and environment model. Results: Across multiple benchmarks, Role-Agent reports an average improvement of more than 4 percent over strong baselines. Conclusion: This is a useful post-training idea because it pushes agent improvement toward self-generated curriculum and environment awareness, not just larger replay buffers. If the approach scales, it could make agent tuning more sample-efficient in domains where real interaction data is expensive or slow to collect.

Method: Monte Carlo Pass Search combines three pieces: a possession-value model, a ball-conditioned world model for multi-agent trajectory rollout, and a counterfactual action policy that samples noisy pass variants. Using a Bundesliga tracking dataset with 3D ball trajectories, it infers kick parameters for each observed pass, rolls out hypothetical continuations until the next ball interaction, and scores the resulting value distribution with mean- and percentile-based execution-surplus metrics. Results: The authors report strong best-of-20 forecasting accuracy versus baselines for the world-model component while also enabling fully hypothetical pass rollouts for downstream analysis.
