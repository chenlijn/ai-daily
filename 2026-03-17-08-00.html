Semantic Invariance in Agentic AI
arXiv:2603.13173 | AMSTA 2026 | J. De Curtò et al.
🎯 Problem

LLM agents deployed in consequential applications require reasoning stability under semantically equivalent input variations, which current benchmark evaluations fail to capture.

🔧 Method

A metamorphic testing framework applying eight semantic-preserving transformations (identity, paraphrase, fact reordering, expansion, contraction, academic context, business context, contrastive formulation) across seven foundation models.

📊 Results

Smaller Qwen3-30B-A3B achieved highest stability (79.6% invariant responses, semantic similarity 0.91), while larger models showed greater fragility, demonstrating model scale does not predict robustness.

💡 Conclusion

Semantic invariance should be a standard evaluation criterion for LLM agents, with metamorphic testing providing systematic assessment of reasoning robustness.

Developing and evaluating a chatbot to support maternal health care
arXiv:2603.13168 | Submitted to IJCAI 2026 | Smriti Jha et al.
🎯 Problem

Deploying trustworthy maternal health chatbots in low-resource settings faces challenges: short underspecified queries, code-mixed languages, regional context requirements, and safe routing with incomplete symptom data.

🔧 Method

Stage-aware triage system combining hybrid retrieval over curated guidelines, evidence-conditioned LLM generation, and multi-method evaluation including labeled triage benchmarks and LLM-as-judge comparisons.

📊 Results

Achieved 86.7% emergency recall on triage benchmark (N=150), with explicit reporting of missed-emergency vs over-escalation trade-offs. Real query evaluation (N=781) using clinician-codesigned criteria demonstrated reliability.

💡 Conclusion

Trustworthy medical assistants in multilingual settings require defense-in-depth design paired with multi-method evaluation rather than any single model choice.

When Right Meets Wrong: Bilateral Context Conditioning with Reward-Confidence Correction for GRPO
arXiv:2603.13134 | Yu Li et al.
🎯 Problem

GRPO treats each output as independent during optimization, overlooking the contrast between correct and incorrect solutions within the same group that could enhance reasoning model training.

🔧 Method

Bilateral Context Conditioning (BICC) allowing cross-referencing of successful and failed reasoning traces; Reward-Confidence Correction (RCC) stabilizes training by adjusting advantage baseline using reward-confidence covariance.

📊 Results

Consistent improvements across mathematical reasoning benchmarks with comprehensive models and algorithms, requiring no additional sampling or auxiliary models.

💡 Conclusion

Explicitly pitting correct against incorrect reasoning traces within GRPO groups unlocks performance gains, with both mechanisms adaptable to all GRPO variants.

Open-World Embodied Self-Evolution via Fine-Grained Diagnosis and Dual-Track Knowledge Distillation
arXiv:2603.13131 | Zhisheng Chen et al.
🎯 Problem

Open-world embodied agents solving long-horizon tasks face bottlenecks not in single-step planning but in organizing and evolving interaction experience effectively.

🔧 Method

Steve-Evolving framework with three phases: Experience Anchoring (structured tuples with multi-dimensional indices), Experience Distillation (skills from successes, guardrails from failures), and Knowledge-Driven Closed-Loop Control (diagnosis-triggered replanning).

📊 Results

Consistent improvements on Minecraft MCU long-horizon suite over static-retrieval baselines through continual evolution without model parameter updates.

💡 Conclusion

Non-parametric self-evolution through fine-grained diagnosis and dual-track distillation enables embodied agents to improve from experience without retraining.

Beyond Final Answers: CRYSTAL Benchmark for Transparent Multimodal Reasoning Evaluation
arXiv:2603.13099 | Wayner Barrios et al.
🎯 Problem

Current MLLM evaluation focuses on final answers, masking systematic failures in reasoning transparency including cherry-picking, non-monotonic scaling, and disordered reasoning chains.

🔧 Method

CRYSTAL benchmark with 6,372 instances evaluating multimodal reasoning through verifiable intermediate steps; Match F1 and Ordered Match F1 metrics; Causal Process Reward (CPR) coupling answer correctness with step-level alignment.

📊 Results

No competitive model preserves more than 60% of matched steps in correct order. CPR-Curriculum achieves +32% Match F1 via GRPO where additive reward strategies fail.

💡 Conclusion

Step-level evaluation reveals critical reasoning failures invisible to accuracy metrics, with curriculum-based process rewards enabling improvement without manual annotation.

Structured Distillation for Personalized Agent Memory: 11x Token Reduction with Retrieval Preservation
arXiv:2603.13017 | Sydney Lewis et al.
🎯 Problem

Carrying full conversation history verbatim with AI agents is expensive; need for compact personalized memory that maintains retrieval quality at reduced token cost.

🔧 Method

Compress exchanges into compound objects (exchange_core, specific_context, thematic_assignments, files_touched); 38 tokens per exchange distilled from 371 tokens average; evaluated with 201 recall-oriented queries across 107 configurations.

📊 Results

11x compression achieved (371→38 tokens). Best distilled configuration reaches 96% of verbatim MRR (0.717 vs 0.745). BM25 degrades significantly while vector search remains stable.

💡 Conclusion

Structured distillation enables thousands of exchanges to fit within a single prompt cost-effectively while preserving retrieval quality, making long-term agent memory practical.

Efficient and Interpretable Multi-Agent LLM Routing via Ant Colony Optimization
arXiv:2603.12933 | Submitted to IEEE TAI | Xudong Wang et al.
🎯 Problem

🔧 Method

AMRO-S framework modeling MAS routing as semantic-conditioned path selection: SFT small LM for intent inference, task-specific pheromone specialists reducing cross-task interference, quality-gated asynchronous updates decoupling inference from learning.

📊 Results

Consistent quality-cost trade-off improvements over strong baselines on five public benchmarks and high-concurrency stress tests, with traceable routing evidence through structured pheromone patterns.

💡 Conclusion

Ant Colony Optimization principles adapted for LLM routing provide efficiency and interpretability simultaneously, addressing key deployment barriers for heterogeneous MAS.

ODRL Policy Comparison Through Normalisation

💡 Conclusion

AI Model Modulation with Logits Redistribution

🔧 Method

💡 Conclusion
