📄 Top 10 arXiv Papers (cs.AI)

Think Before You Lie: How Reasoning Improves Honesty
arXiv:2603.09957 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Existing evaluations measure deception rates but don't understand the underlying conditions causing deceptive behavior in LLMs. The relationship between reasoning and honesty is poorly understood.
⚙️ Method: Created a novel dataset of realistic moral trade-offs where honesty incurs variable costs. Investigated how reasoning affects honesty across multiple LLM families and scales.
📊 Results: Contrary to humans (who become less honest with deliberation), reasoning consistently increases honesty in LLMs across all scales. Deceptive regions in representational space are metastable and more easily destabilized than honest ones.
✅ Conclusion: Generating deliberative tokens during moral reasoning traverses a biased representational space, nudging models toward more stable, honest defaults. This provides insights for monitoring and improving LLM honesty.
The Confidence Gate Theorem: When Should Ranked Decision Systems Abstain?
arXiv:2603.09947 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Ranked decision systems (recommenders, ad auctions, clinical triage) must decide when to intervene versus abstain, but confidence-based abstention doesn't always improve decision quality.
⚙️ Method: Formalized conditions for monotonic abstention gains (rank-alignment and no inversion zones). Distinguished between structural uncertainty (missing data) and contextual uncertainty (missing context/temporal drift).
📊 Results: Validated across collaborative filtering (MovieLens), e-commerce intent detection (RetailRocket, Criteo), and clinical triage (MIMIC-IV). Structural uncertainty produces near-monotonic gains; contextual uncertainty causes violations comparable to random abstention.
✅ Conclusion: Context-aware alternatives (ensemble disagreement, recency features) reduce violations but don't fully restore monotonicity. Provides practical deployment diagnostics for matching confidence signals to uncertainty types.
Toward Cognition-Aligned Memory Transformation for Pathology MLLMs
arXiv:2603.09943 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Multimodal LLMs lack explicit mechanisms for structured pathology knowledge integration. They struggle to consistently incorporate diagnostic standards during reasoning despite strong vision-language capabilities.
⚙️ Method: Proposed PathMem, a memory-centric framework inspired by human pathologist cognitive processes. Introduces Memory Transformer modeling dynamic transition from long-term to working memory through multimodal activation.
📊 Results: Achieves SOTA performance across benchmarks. Improved WSI-Bench report generation by 12.8% (precision) and 10.1% (relevance). Open-ended diagnosis improved by 9.7% and 8.9% over prior WSI-based models.
✅ Conclusion: Cognition-aligned memory transformation enables context-aware knowledge grounding for pathology diagnosis. This approach bridges the gap between general vision-language models and domain-specific medical applications.
A Unified Orchestration Framework for Benchmarking Multimodal Medical Multi-Agent Systems
arXiv:2603.09909 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Medical Multi-Agent Systems research suffers from architectural fragmentation, non-uniform data ingestion, inconsistent visual-reasoning evaluation, and lack of cross-specialty benchmarking.
⚙️ Method: Introduced MedMASLab with three innovations: standardized multimodal agent communication protocol (11 architectures, 24 modalities), zero-shot semantic evaluator using vision-language models, and comprehensive benchmark spanning 11 organ systems and 473 diseases.
📊 Results: Revealed critical domain-specific performance gaps: MAS improves reasoning depth but exhibits fragility when transitioning between medical sub-domains. Systematic ablation of interaction mechanisms and cost-performance trade-offs established new baselines.
✅ Conclusion: Provides rigorous foundation for autonomous clinical systems with standardized evaluation. The most extensive medical MAS benchmark to date establishes technical baselines for future research in clinical decision support.
Influencing LLM Multi-Agent Dialogue via Policy-Parameterized Prompts
arXiv:2603.09890 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Research on LLM-based multi-agent behavior relies on ad hoc prompts lacking principled policy perspectives. No systematic approach exists for influencing conversational dynamics without training.
⚙️ Method: Treats prompts as actions executed by LLMs, dynamically constructing prompts through five state-based components. Evaluated dialogue flow using responsiveness, rebuttal, evidence usage, non-repetition, and stance shift indicators.
📊 Results: Policy-parameterized prompts successfully influence dialogue dynamics across different LLM-driven agents in public discussion scenarios. Demonstrates lightweight control mechanism without requiring model retraining.
✅ Conclusion: Prompt-as-action parameterization offers simple and effective mechanism to influence multi-agent dialogue processes. Enables social simulation research with controllable agent behaviors for studying group dynamics.
Local Classifier Alignment for Continual Learning
arXiv:2603.09888 | Accepted to ICLR 2026
🔍 Problem: Continual learning with pre-trained models suffers from catastrophic forgetting. Existing approaches create mismatches between task-specific classifiers and adapted backbones as more tasks arrive.
⚙️ Method: Proposed Local Classifier Alignment (LCA) loss to better align classifiers with backbones. Theoretically showed LCA enables generalization across observed tasks while improving robustness.
📊 Results: Extensive experiments on standard benchmarks demonstrate leading performance, sometimes surpassing state-of-the-art by large margins. Complete solution developed following model merging approach with LCA.
✅ Conclusion: LCA addresses the classifier-backbone mismatch in continual learning, providing a principled approach for intelligent systems to learn continuously under changing environments without catastrophic forgetting.
Quantifying the Necessity of Chain of Thought through Opaque Serial Depth
arXiv:2603.09786 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Understanding when and why chain-of-thought reasoning is necessary for LLMs is crucial for monitoring and interpretability. Long serial cognition may happen without interpretable intermediate steps.
⚙️ Method: Formalized "opaque serial depth" as the longest computation possible without interpretable intermediate steps. Computed upper bounds for Gemma 3 models and developed open-source automated calculation method for arbitrary neural networks.
📊 Results: Demonstrated that Mixture-of-Experts models likely have lower opaque serial depth than dense models. Quantified upper bounds showing when models must externalize reasoning versus when hidden computation is possible.
✅ Conclusion: Opaque serial depth is a useful tool for understanding models' potential for hidden reasoning. This framework helps assess the monitoring implications of different architectures and their transparency characteristics.
Cognition Toolkit for Allocentric Spatial Reasoning in Foundation Models
arXiv:2603.09774 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Multimodal Foundation Models struggle with robust spatial reasoning, either overfitting statistical shortcuts via 3D grounding data or remaining confined to 2D perception, limiting accuracy and generalization.
⚙️ Method: Proposed World2Mind, a training-free spatial intelligence toolkit inspired by biological cognitive mapping. Constructs Allocentric-Spatial Trees (AST) using elliptical parameters for accurate top-down landmark layouts.
📊 Results: Boosts frontier models like GPT-5.2 performance by 5-18%. Astonishingly, purely text-only foundation models using AST-structured text can perform complex 3D spatial reasoning approaching advanced multimodal model performance.
✅ Conclusion: Structured spatial cognitive maps enable MFMs to proactively acquire targeted spatial knowledge. Three-stage reasoning chain mitigates 3D reconstruction inaccuracies, advancing allocentric spatial reasoning capabilities.
Evolving Cognition and Elastic Memory Orchestration for Adaptive Agents
arXiv:2603.09716 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: Autonomous agent frameworks struggle to reconcile long-term experiential learning with real-time context-sensitive decision-making, resulting in static cognition, rigid workflows, and inefficient context usage.
⚙️ Method: Presented AutoAgent with three components: evolving cognition (structured prompt-level knowledge), on-the-fly contextual decision-making, and Elastic Memory Orchestrator (compresses trajectories, constructs episodic abstractions).
📊 Results: Consistently improves task success, tool-use efficiency, and collaborative robustness over static and memory-augmented baselines across retrieval-augmented reasoning, tool-augmented benchmarks, and embodied tasks.
✅ Conclusion: Closed-loop cognitive evolution aligns intended actions with observed outcomes to continuously update cognition without external retraining. Provides unified foundation for adaptive agents in dynamic environments.
Evaluating Genuine Reasoning in Large Language Models via Esoteric Programming Languages
arXiv:2603.09678 | Tue, 10 Mar 2026
🔍 Problem: LLMs achieve near-ceiling performance on code benchmarks, but results increasingly reflect memorization rather than genuine reasoning. Standard benchmarks have become contaminated with training data.
⚙️ Method: Introduced EsoLang-Bench using five esoteric languages (Brainfuck, Befunge-98, Whitespace, Unlambda, Shakespeare) with 1,000-100,000x fewer public repositories than Python, making them economically irrational for pre-training.
📊 Results: Models achieving 85-95% on standard benchmarks score only 0-11% on equivalent esoteric tasks, with 0% accuracy beyond Easy tier. Few-shot learning and self-reflection fail to improve performance, suggesting exploitation of training priors rather than genuine learning.
✅ Conclusion: EsoLang-Bench provides the first benchmark designed to mimic human learning through documentation, interpreter feedback, and iterative experimentation. Measures transferable reasoning skills resistant to data contamination.
