📄 Top 10 arXiv Papers (cs.AI)

[2603.09957] Think Before You Lie: How Reasoning Improves Honesty
Problem: While LLM deception rates are measured, the underlying conditions causing deceptive behavior remain poorly understood.
Method: Created a novel dataset of realistic moral trade-offs where honesty incurs variable costs, analyzing reasoning's effect on LLM honesty across scales and model families.
Results: Contrary to humans who become less honest with deliberation, reasoning consistently increases LLM honesty. Deceptive regions in representational space are metastable and more easily destabilized than honest ones.
Conclusion: Generating deliberative tokens during moral reasoning traverses a biased representational space, nudging models toward more stable, honest defaults.
[2603.09947] The Confidence Gate Theorem: When Should Ranked Decision Systems Abstain?
Problem: Ranked decision systems (recommenders, ad auctions, clinical triage) need principled abstention mechanisms but lack formal conditions for when confidence-based abstention improves decision quality.
Method: Formalized conditions (rank-alignment and no inversion zones) and validated across collaborative filtering, e-commerce, and clinical pathways, distinguishing structural vs contextual uncertainty.
Results: Structural uncertainty produces near-monotonic abstention gains; context-aware confidence signals reduce monotonicity violations from 3 to 1-2 compared to exception-based methods that degrade under distribution shift.
Conclusion: Confidence gates should match the confidence signal to the dominant uncertainty type and validate conditions on held-out data before deployment.
[2603.09943] Toward Cognition-Aligned Memory Transformation for Pathology MLLMs
Problem: MLLMs lack explicit mechanisms for structured knowledge integration in pathology, struggling to incorporate diagnostic standards during reasoning.
Method: Proposed PathMem with Memory Transformer modeling transitions from long-term to working memory through multimodal activation and context-aware knowledge grounding.
Results: Achieved SOTA on WSI-Bench with 12.8% WSI-Precision improvement and 10.1% WSI-Relevance gains over prior WSI-based models.
Conclusion: Cognition-aligned memory transformation enables pathology MLLMs to effectively integrate structured domain knowledge for improved diagnostic reasoning.
[2603.09909] A Unified Orchestration Framework for Benchmarking Multimodal Medical Multi-Agent Systems
Problem: Medical MAS research suffers from architectural fragmentation, non-uniform data pipelines, and lack of standardized multimodal integration and cross-specialty benchmarking.
Method: Introduced MedMASLab with standardized multimodal agent communication protocol, zero-shot semantic evaluation paradigm, and benchmark spanning 11 organ systems and 473 diseases.
Results: Revealed critical domain-specific performance gaps where MAS architectures exhibit fragility when transitioning between medical sub-domains despite improved reasoning depth.
Conclusion: Standardized benchmarking and rigorous ablation of interaction mechanisms establish a new technical baseline for autonomous clinical systems.
[2603.09890] Influencing LLM Multi-Agent Dialogue via Policy-Parameterized Prompts
Problem: LLM-based multi-agent research relies on ad hoc prompts without principled policy perspectives for influencing conversational behaviors.
Method: Proposed treating prompts as parameterized actions through five components based on agent state, evaluated via responsiveness, rebuttal, evidence usage, non-repetition, and stance shift metrics.
Results: Prompt parameterization successfully influences dialogue dynamics in public discussion scenarios without requiring model training.
Conclusion: Policy-parameterized prompts offer a simple, effective mechanism to influence multi-agent dialogue processes for social simulation research.
[2603.09888] Local Classifier Alignment for Continual Learning
Problem: Pre-trained models for continual learning create potential mismatch between task-specific classifiers and adapted backbones as tasks accumulate.
Method: Proposed Local Classifier Alignment (LCA) loss to align classifiers with backbones, theoretically showing improved generalization and robustness.
Results: Achieved leading performance on standard benchmarks, sometimes surpassing state-of-the-art with large margins using the model merging approach with LCA.
Conclusion: Local classifier alignment effectively addresses the backbone-classifier mismatch in continual learning with pre-trained models.
[2603.09786] Quantifying the Necessity of Chain of Thought through Opaque Serial Depth
Problem: Understanding how much reasoning can occur without interpretable intermediate steps like chain of thought remains unclear for LLM monitoring.
Method: Formalized opaque serial depth as the longest computation without interpretable steps, computing bounds for Gemma 3 and developing automated calculation methods for arbitrary neural networks.
Results: Mixture-of-Experts models likely have lower opaque depth than dense models; numeric upper bounds established for Gemma 3 architecture variants.
Conclusion: Opaque serial depth is a useful tool for understanding models' potential for hidden reasoning not externalized in chain of thought.
[2603.09774] Cognition Toolkit for Allocentric Spatial Reasoning in Foundation Models
Problem: Current multimodal foundation models overfit statistical shortcuts or remain confined to 2D perception, limiting spatial reasoning accuracy and generalization.
Method: Proposed World2Mind toolkit using 3D reconstruction and instance segmentation to construct Allocentric-Spatial Trees with elliptical parameters modeling landmark layouts.
Results: Boosted GPT-5.2 performance by 5-18%; purely text-only models using AST-structured text approached advanced multimodal model performance on 3D spatial reasoning.
Conclusion: Structured spatial cognitive maps enable proactive knowledge acquisition and robust geometric-topological reasoning in foundation models.
[2603.09716] Evolving Cognition and Elastic Memory Orchestration for Adaptive Agents
Problem: Agent frameworks struggle to reconcile long-term experiential learning with real-time context-sensitive decision-making, showing static cognition and rigid workflow dependence.
Method: Developed AutoAgent with evolving cognition, on-the-fly contextual decision-making, and elastic memory orchestration organizing history through compression and episodic abstractions.
Results: Improved task success, tool-use efficiency, and collaborative robustness over static and memory-augmented baselines across retrieval, tool-augmented, and embodied benchmarks.
Conclusion: Closed-loop cognitive evolution provides a unified foundation for adaptive agents learning from experience while making reliable context-aware decisions.
[2603.09715] Does the Question Really Matter? Training-Free Data Selection for Vision-Language SFT
Problem: Visual instruction tuning samples often rely on linguistic patterns without genuine cross-modal reasoning, and data selection methods require costly proxy model training.
Method: Proposed CVS (Conditional Validity Shift) measuring discrepancy in answer validity with/without question conditioning using frozen VLLM as evaluator.
Results: Outperformed full-data training by 3.5% and 4.8% using only 10% and 15% of Vision-Flan data; reduced computational cost by 17.3% and 44.4% compared to COINCIDE and XMAS.
Conclusion: Training-free data selection based on question-conditional validity shifts effectively identifies samples requiring genuine vision-language joint reasoning.
