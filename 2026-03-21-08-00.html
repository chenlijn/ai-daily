1. A Scalable Critic Framework for Generalist GUI Rewards
arXiv:2603.19191 | March 20, 2026

🔍 Problem

Reinforcement Learning for GUI agents struggles with reward quality - existing approaches can't achieve both scalability and performance simultaneously in stochastic environments.

⚙️ Method

OS-Themis framework introduces a multi-agent critic that decomposes trajectories into verifiable milestones with a review mechanism for evidence chain auditing before final verdict.

📊 Results

10.3% improvement in online RL training on AndroidWorld, 6.9% gain in self-training loop validation, with all models achieving best performance under OS-Themis on OGRBench.

✅ Conclusion

OS-Themis demonstrates scalable and accurate reward evaluation, highlighting potential to drive significant evolution in GUI agent capabilities through better reward modeling.

2. A Process-Control Architecture for Reliable LLM Reasoning
arXiv:2603.19182 | March 19, 2026

🔍 Problem

LLMs remain vulnerable to hallucination and unreliable reasoning under adversarial prompting, with existing safety approaches lacking explicit architectural mechanisms for reasoning integrity.

⚙️ Method

Box Maze framework decomposes LLM reasoning into three layers: memory grounding, structured inference, and boundary enforcement, with simulation-based evaluation across DeepSeek-V3, Doubao, and Qwen.

📊 Results

Architectural constraints reduced boundary failure rates from ~40% (baseline RLHF) to below 1% under adversarial conditions across 50 adversarial scenarios.

✅ Conclusion

Process-level control offers a promising direction for improving LLM reasoning reliability, suggesting explicit cognitive control layers may significantly enhance consistency.

3. A GPU-Accelerated General-Purpose Metaheuristic Framework for Combinatorial Optimization
arXiv:2603.19163 | March 19, 2026

🔍 Problem

Combinatorial optimization faces a fundamental trade-off among generality, performance, and usability - existing approaches excel in one dimension at the expense of others.

⚙️ Method

cuGenOpt introduces a "one block evolves one solution" CUDA architecture with unified encoding abstraction, two-level adaptive operator selection, and an LLM-based modeling assistant for natural-language problem descriptions.

📊 Results

Outperforms general MIP solvers by orders of magnitude, achieves 4.73% gap on TSP-442 within 30s, reduces pcb442 gap from 36% to 4.73%, boosts VRPTW throughput by 75-81%.

✅ Conclusion

cuGenOpt successfully addresses all three dimensions simultaneously, offering a pure-Python API with JIT compilation that makes GPU-accelerated optimization accessible to domain experts.

4. Partition Determinantal Point Process for Diversity in Parallel Discrete Diffusion Decoding
arXiv:2603.19146 | March 19, 2026

🔍 Problem

Discrete diffusion models show promise for text generation, but their decoding methods are under-studied, with existing techniques providing limited control over in-batch diversity.

⚙️ Method

D5P4 introduces a generalized beam-search framework for discrete diffusion that generates candidates in parallel, formulating selection as MAP inference over a Determinantal Point Process with a scalable greedy solver.

📊 Results

D5P4 improves diversity over strong baselines while maintaining competitive generation quality on free-form generation and question answering tasks, with near-zero compute overhead.

✅ Conclusion

The approach enables explicit trade-off between model probability and target diversity, making discrete diffusion models more practical for diverse text generation applications.

5. Implicit Patterns in LLM-Based Binary Analysis
arXiv:2603.19138 | March 20, 2026

🔍 Problem

How LLM-based systems organize exploration over hundreds of reasoning steps in binary vulnerability analysis remains poorly understood due to limited context windows and implicit token-level behaviors.

⚙️ Method

Large-scale trace-level study analyzing 521 binaries with 99,563 reasoning steps to identify structured, token-level implicit patterns in multi-pass LLM reasoning systems.

📊 Results

Identified four dominant patterns: early pruning, path-dependent lock-in, targeted backtracking, and knowledge-guided prioritization that form a stable, structured system with distinct temporal roles.

✅ Conclusion

These token-level patterns provide the first systematic characterization of LLM-driven binary analysis, establishing a foundation for developing more reliable automated vulnerability analysis systems.

6. How Uncertainty Estimation Scales with Sampling in Reasoning Models
arXiv:2603.19118 | March 19, 2026

🔍 Problem

Uncertainty estimation for reasoning language models remains poorly understood under extended chain-of-thought reasoning, particularly how parallel sampling scales as a black-box approach.

⚙️ Method

Study across three reasoning models and 17 tasks spanning mathematics, STEM, and humanities using verbalized confidence and self-consistency as uncertainty signals.

📊 Results

Hybrid estimator with just two samples improves AUROC by up to +12 points and outperforms either signal alone at larger budgets; effects are domain-dependent with mathematics showing stronger complementarity.

✅ Conclusion

Signal combination offers the most effective uncertainty estimation strategy, with implications for deploying reasoning models in high-stakes applications requiring calibrated confidence.

7. Latent Unified Mamba for Electrode Topology-Invariant and Efficient EEG Modeling
arXiv:2603.19100 | March 19, 2026

🔍 Problem

Building foundation models for EEG is challenging due to differing electrode topologies and computational scalability issues, as Transformer architectures incur quadratic sequence complexity.

⚙️ Method

LuMamba combines topology-invariant encodings with linear-complexity state-space modeling using LUNA's learned-query cross-attention for channel unification and FEMBA's bidirectional Mamba blocks for temporal modeling.

📊 Results

With only 4.6M parameters, LuMamba attains 80.99% balanced accuracy on TUAB and achieves state-of-the-art performance on Alzheimer's detection (0.97 AUPR), requiring 377x fewer FLOPs than SOTA models.

✅ Conclusion

The LeJEPA pre-training approach combined with masked reconstruction achieves the most robust performance, demonstrating that efficient state-space models can match or exceed Transformer performance on biosignal tasks.

8. Serendipity by Design: Evaluating the Impact of Cross-domain Mappings on Human and LLM Creativity
arXiv:2603.19087 | March 19, 2026

🔍 Problem

Are LLMs creative in the same way humans are, and can the same interventions increase creativity in both? Cross-domain mapping as a creativity intervention remains largely untested for LLMs.

⚙️ Method

Human participants and LLMs generated novel features for daily products under cross-domain mapping prompts (translating properties from random sources like octopus, cactus, GPS) versus user-need prompts.

📊 Results

Humans reliably benefit from randomly assigned cross-domain mappings, while LLMs generate more original ideas on average but do not show statistically significant effects from the intervention; impact increases with semantic distance.

✅ Conclusion

Results highlight both the role of remote association in creative ideation and systematic differences in how humans and LLMs respond to creativity interventions, suggesting different optimization strategies for each.

10. Behavioral Fingerprints for LLM Endpoint Stability and Identity
arXiv:2603.19022 | March 19, 2026

🔍 Problem

Traditional reliability metrics (uptime, latency, throughput) don't capture behavioral change in AI-native applications; endpoints can remain "healthy" while their effective model identity changes due to various updates.

⚙️ Method

Stability Monitor system periodically fingerprints endpoints by sampling outputs from fixed prompt sets and comparing output distributions over time using summed energy distance statistics with sequential aggregation.

📊 Results

Detects changes to model family, version, inference stack, quantization, and behavioral parameters; real-world monitoring reveals substantial provider-to-provider and within-provider stability differences for the same model.

✅ Conclusion

Behavioral fingerprinting provides a necessary complement to traditional reliability metrics, enabling detection of model identity drift that would otherwise go unnoticed in production AI systems.
