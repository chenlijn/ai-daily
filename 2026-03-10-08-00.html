1. Schema-Gated Agentic AI for Flexible and Reproducible Scientific Workflows

arXiv: 2603.06394 | Subjects: AI, ML, Multi-Agent Systems

  • Problem: Scientific workflows require deterministic execution and provenance tracking, but LLM-based systems struggle to balance conversational flexibility with reproducible outputs.
  • Method: Introduces schema-gated orchestration where execution plans must validate against machine-checkable specifications before running. Uses multi-model scoring (15 sessions across 3 LLM families) for architectural assessment.
  • Results: Demonstrates substantial-to-near-perfect inter-model agreement (Krippendorff α=0.80 for execution determinism, α=0.98 for conversational flexibility).
  • Conclusion: Schema-gated architecture successfully decouples the flexibility-determinism trade-off, providing 3 operational principles for adoption: clarification-before-execution, constrained plan-act orchestration, and tool-to-workflow-level gating.

URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06394

2. Safeguarded Alignment for High-Order Optimization in Recursive Self-Improvement (SAHOO)

arXiv: 2603.06333 | Subjects: AI, NLP, ML | Published: ICLR 2026 Workshop

  • Problem: Recursive self-improvement in AI systems risks alignment drift during iterative self-modification cycles.
  • Method: Introduces SAHOO framework with three safeguards: Goal Drift Index (GDI) combining semantic/lexical/structural/distributional measures, constraint preservation checks, and regression-risk quantification.
  • Results: Achieves 18.3% improvement in code tasks and 16.8% in mathematical reasoning while preserving constraints, validated across 189 tasks with only 18 validation tasks for threshold calibration.
  • Conclusion: Makes alignment preservation during recursive self-improvement measurable and systematically deployable, exposing domain-specific tensions like fluency vs. factuality.

URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06333

4. Distilling Semantic Knowledge from LLMs into Bird's-Eye View Representations (BEVLM)

arXiv: 2603.06576 | Subjects: CV, AI, ML, Robotics

  • Problem: LLMs in autonomous driving process multi-view tokens independently, causing redundant computation and limited spatial consistency; BEV representations lack semantic richness.
  • Method: BEVLM bridges spatially-consistent BEV representations with LLMs through knowledge distillation from LLMs into BEV representations.
  • Results: Improves cross-view reasoning accuracy by 46% and closed-loop end-to-end driving performance by 29% in safety-critical scenarios.
  • Conclusion: Unified BEV features enable more effective LLM reasoning in autonomous driving scenarios.
