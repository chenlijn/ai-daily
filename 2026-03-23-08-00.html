A Scalable Critic Framework for Generalist GUI Rewards
arXiv:2603.19191 • Zehao Li et al.
🎯 Problem: Reinforcement Learning training for GUI agents is highly sensitive to reward function quality. Existing reward approaches struggle to achieve both scalability and performance.
🔬 Method: OS-Themis - a multi-agent critic framework that decomposes trajectories into verifiable milestones and employs a review mechanism to audit evidence chains before final verdicts. Introduces OmniGUIRewardBench (OGRBench) for evaluation.
📊 Results: 10.3% improvement in online RL training on AndroidWorld; 6.9% gain for trajectory validation in self-training loops. All models achieve best performance under OS-Themis framework.
💡 Conclusion: Multi-agent critic frameworks with milestone decomposition offer a promising direction for improving GUI agent training through better reward modeling.
A Process-Control Architecture for Reliable LLM Reasoning
arXiv:2603.19182 • Qiang Zou et al.
🎯 Problem: LLMs demonstrate strong generative capabilities but remain vulnerable to hallucination and unreliable reasoning under adversarial prompting. Existing safety approaches primarily operate at the behavioral level.
🔬 Method: Box Maze framework - decomposes LLM reasoning into three explicit layers: memory grounding, structured inference, and boundary enforcement. Evaluated on DeepSeek-V3, Doubao, and Qwen across 50 adversarial scenarios.
📊 Results: Architectural constraints reduce boundary failure rates from ~40% (baseline RLHF) to below 1% under adversarial conditions.
💡 Conclusion: Process-level cognitive control layers offer a promising architectural approach for improving LLM reasoning reliability beyond behavioral-level interventions.
A GPU-Accelerated General-Purpose Metaheuristic Framework for Combinatorial Optimization
arXiv:2603.19163 • Yuyang Liu et al.
🎯 Problem: Combinatorial optimization problems face a fundamental trade-off among generality, performance, and usability. Existing approaches cannot address all three dimensions simultaneously.
🔬 Method: cuGenOpt - a GPU-accelerated metaheuristic framework with "one block evolves one solution" CUDA architecture, unified encoding abstraction, two-level adaptive operator selection, and LLM-based modeling assistant for natural-language problem descriptions.
📊 Results: Outperforms general MIP solvers by orders of magnitude; achieves 4.73% gap on TSP-442 within 30s; 12 problem types solved to optimality; VRPTW throughput improved by 75-81%.
💡 Conclusion: GPU-accelerated metaheuristics with unified abstractions and LLM-assisted modeling can democratize access to high-performance combinatorial optimization across diverse problem domains.
Partition Determinantal Point Process for Diversity in Parallel Discrete Diffusion Decoding
arXiv:2603.19146 • Jonathan Lys et al.
🎯 Problem: Discrete diffusion models show promise for text generation but their decoding methods remain under-studied. Standard autoregressive decoding methods don't apply to iterative denoising, and existing techniques provide limited in-batch diversity control.
🔬 Method: D5P4 - a generalized beam-search framework for discrete diffusion that generates candidates in parallel. Uses Determinantal Point Process for diversity-focused selection with a scalable greedy solver, enabling explicit probability-diversity trade-off with near-zero overhead.
📊 Results: Improves diversity over strong baselines while maintaining competitive generation quality on free-form generation and question answering tasks.
💡 Conclusion: Diversity-aware decoding through DPP-based selection offers a practical solution for controlling output diversity in discrete diffusion models for text generation.
Implicit Patterns in LLM-Based Binary Analysis
arXiv:2603.19138 • Qiang Li et al.
🎯 Problem: Multi-pass LLM reasoning for binary vulnerability analysis is poorly understood due to limited context windows and implicit token-level behaviors spanning hundreds of reasoning steps.
🔬 Method: First large-scale trace-level study analyzing 521 binaries with 99,563 reasoning steps to identify token-level implicit patterns in LLM-driven binary analysis.
📊 Results: Identified four dominant patterns: early pruning, path-dependent lock-in, targeted backtracking, and knowledge-guided prioritization. These patterns form a stable, structured system with distinct temporal roles and measurable characteristics.
💡 Conclusion: Token-level implicit patterns serve as a new abstraction for understanding LLM reasoning, organizing exploration through implicit decisions rather than explicit control-flow or heuristics.
How Uncertainty Estimation Scales with Sampling in Reasoning Models
arXiv:2603.19118 • Maksym Del et al.
🎯 Problem: Uncertainty estimation for reasoning language models remains poorly understood under extended chain-of-thought reasoning, critical for safe deployment.
🔬 Method: Study parallel sampling as a black-box approach using verbalized confidence and self-consistency across three reasoning models and 17 tasks spanning mathematics, STEM, and humanities.
📊 Results: Both signals scale in reasoning models; hybrid estimator improves AUROC by up to +12 with just two samples and outperforms either signal alone at larger budgets. Mathematics shows stronger complementarity than STEM or humanities.
💡 Conclusion: Hybrid uncertainty estimation through signal combination offers significant gains over single-signal approaches, with domain-dependent scaling characteristics.
Latent Unified Mamba for Electrode Topology-Invariant and Efficient EEG Modeling
arXiv:2603.19100 • Thorir Mar Ingolfsson et al.
🎯 Problem: Building foundation models for EEG is challenging due to differing electrode topologies and computational scalability, as Transformers incur quadratic sequence complexity.
🔬 Method: LuMamba (Latent Unified Mamba) - combines topology-invariant encodings with linear-complexity state-space modeling using LUNA's learned-query cross-attention and FEMBA's bidirectional Mamba blocks. First systematic investigation of LeJEPA for biosignal learning.
📊 Results: 80.99% balanced accuracy on TUAB; state-of-the-art on Alzheimer's detection (0.97 AUPR); 377× fewer FLOPs than state-of-the-art; scales to 12× longer sequences before GPU memory limits.
💡 Conclusion: Combining masked reconstruction with LeJEPA objectives and Mamba-based architectures enables efficient, scalable foundation models for EEG with only 4.6M parameters.
Serendipity by Design: Evaluating the Impact of Cross-domain Mappings on Human and LLM Creativity
arXiv:2603.19087 • Qiawen Liu et al.
🎯 Problem: Are LLMs creative in the same way humans are, and can the same interventions increase creativity in both? Cross-domain mapping as a creativity intervention remains largely untested.
🔬 Method: Evaluated human participants and LLMs generating novel features for daily products under cross-domain mapping (random source domain) vs. user-need prompts.
📊 Results: Humans reliably benefit from randomly assigned cross-domain mappings; LLMs generate more original ideas on average but don't show significant effects from cross-domain mappings. Impact increases with semantic distance in both systems. 💡 Conclusion: Remote association plays a role in creative ideation, but humans and LLMs respond differently to identical creativity interventions, highlighting systematic differences in creative processes.
Artificial Intelligence and Judicial Decision Making
arXiv:2603.19042 • Arthur Dyevre et al.
🎯 Problem: Integration of AI into judicial decision-making raises concerns about transparency, reliability, and accountability, yet empirical evidence on AI's impact remains limited.
🔬 Method: Synthetic review connecting AI tool performance/fairness, human judge biases, and AI+human interactions across computer science, economics, law, criminology, and psychology literatures.
📊 Results: Existing evidence indicates modest or nonexistent impact of AI decision aids on pretrial and sentencing decisions. Significant gaps remain in understanding how judges navigate noisy environments and respond to AI advice.
💡 Conclusion: AI vs. human comparisons can yield insights into both algorithmic tools and human decision-makers; greater interdisciplinary integration is needed for judicial AI research.
Behavioral Fingerprints for LLM Endpoint Stability and Identity
arXiv:2603.19022 • Jonah Leshin et al.
🎯 Problem: Traditional reliability metrics (uptime, latency, throughput) don't capture behavioral changes in AI-native applications. Endpoints can remain "healthy" while model identity changes due to updates.
🔬 Method: Stability Monitor - black-box stability monitoring system that fingerprints endpoints by sampling outputs from fixed prompts and comparing output distributions over time using summed energy distance with sequential aggregation.
📊 Results: Detects changes to model family, version, inference stack, quantization, and behavioral parameters. Substantial provider-to-provider and within-provider stability differences observed in real-world monitoring.
💡 Conclusion: Behavioral fingerprinting enables detection of subtle model changes that traditional health checks miss, providing crucial stability monitoring for production AI systems.
