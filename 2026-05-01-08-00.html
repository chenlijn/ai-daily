Problem: Claw-style personal agents must operate over files, tools, and persistent workspaces, but the field lacks enough realistic, verifiable training tasks and stable benchmarks to improve them systematically. Method: ClawGym builds the full pipeline: a 13.5K-task synthetic dataset generated from both persona-driven intents and skill-grounded task composition, workspace construction with realistic files and resources, hybrid verification using code checkers plus rubric judges, and training procedures that combine black-box rollout collection, SFT, and sandbox-parallel RL. Results: The paper reports substantial gains from synthetic task training. Method: Bian Que frames operations work through three recurring modes: release interception, proactive inspection, and alert RCA. Conclusion: This paper stands out for connecting agent orchestration directly to measurable SRE and operations value. Method: Using a 21-day deployment of DX Terminal Pro, the authors instrument the full control loop around the model: typed user controls, prompt compilation, policy validators, execution guards, memory semantics, and trace-level observability. 7. Distill-Belief: Closed-Loop Inverse Source Localization and Characterization in Physical Fields

arXiv: 2604.26095 2026-05-01 Heat Score: 62 (Watchlist) HF Trending: No | HF Upvotes: 0

Why it matters now: It matches today’s hot direction(s): efficiency, agent, post_training. Its direction is also showing up in today’s GitHub tooling and implementation activity. It overlaps with what the builder community is actively discussing today. It has relatively direct relevance to production workflows or enterprise deployment scenarios.

Problem: In inverse source localization, a mobile agent must decide where to measure next while also inferring hidden source parameters under tight sensing budgets. Pure Bayesian filtering is accurate but expensive, while learned belief models are cheap but can be exploited by reinforcement learning policies that game the approximation instead of truly reducing uncertainty.

Method: Distill-Belief uses a teacher-student design. A particle-filter teacher maintains a Bayes-correct posterior and provides an information-gain signal during training, while a compact student learns distilled belief statistics plus an uncertainty-based stopping certificate; at deployment, only the student runs, giving near-constant per-step cost.

Results: Across seven field modalities plus two stress-test settings, the paper reports lower sensing cost and better success, posterior contraction, and parameter-estimation accuracy than baseline approaches. The extracted text does not provide a compact single headline number, but it does make clear the gains hold across multiple environments and that the method reduces reward hacking.

Conclusion: The paper is interesting beyond this domain because it shows a practical recipe for combining principled inference with deployable control. For embodied or scientific agents, it is a nice example of how to keep uncertainty honest without paying full Bayesian cost online.
