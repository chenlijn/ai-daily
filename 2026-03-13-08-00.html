A Hybrid Knowledge-Grounded Framework for Safety and Traceability in Prescription Verification
arXiv:2603.10891 | cs.AI

Problem

Medication errors threaten patient safety, and pharmacist verification is heavily burdened. Direct LLM application is untenable due to factual unreliability and lack of traceability.

Method

PharmGraph-Auditor with Hybrid Pharmaceutical Knowledge Base (HPKB) using Virtual Knowledge Graph paradigm, Iterative Schema Refinement (ISR) algorithm, and KB-grounded Chain of Verification (CoV).

Results

Demonstrates robust knowledge extraction and enables safer, faster prescription verification for pharmacists through transparent reasoning.

Conclusion

Hybrid knowledge-grounded reasoning transforms LLMs from unreliable generators into transparent, evidence-based reasoning engines for critical healthcare applications.

Nurture-First Agent Development: Building Domain-Expert AI Agents Through Conversational Knowledge Crystallization
arXiv:2603.10808 | cs.AI

Problem

Traditional code-first and prompt-first agent development paradigms treat expertise as static, creating mismatch with evolving, tacit domain knowledge.

Method

Nurture-First Development (NFD) with Knowledge Crystallization Cycle, Three-Layer Cognitive Architecture, Dual-Workspace Pattern, and Spiral Development Model.

Results

Case study on financial research agent demonstrates progressive growth through structured conversational interaction with domain practitioners.

Conclusion

Agents should be initialized with minimal scaffolding and grown through conversation, enabling human-agent co-evolution for domain expertise.

Emulating Clinician Cognition via Self-Evolving Deep Clinical Research
arXiv:2603.10677 | cs.AI

Problem

Current AI systems treat diagnosis as single-pass prediction, lacking dynamic cue acquisition and auditable mechanisms for governed improvement.

Method

DxEvolve framework with interactive deep clinical research workflow, autonomous examination requisition, and externalization of clinical experience as diagnostic cognition primitives.

Results

11.2% improvement over backbone models on MIMIC-CDM, reaching 90.4% accuracy on reader-study subset, comparable to clinician reference (88.8%).

Conclusion

Transforming clinical experience into governable learning assets enables accountable, continual evolution of clinical AI systems.

Formal Abstract Minimal Explanation for Neural Networks
arXiv:2603.10661 | cs.AI

Problem

Neural network explanations often lack formal guarantees and scale poorly to large networks, making them unreliable for high-stakes decisions.

Method

FAME (Formal Abstract Minimal Explanations) using abstract interpretation, dedicated perturbation domains, and LiRPA-based bounds to converge to formal abstract minimal explanations.

Results

First method to scale to large neural networks while reducing explanation size; demonstrates consistent gains in explanation size and runtime over VERIX+.

Conclusion

Formal abstract interpretation enables scalable, verifiable neural network explanations suitable for safety-critical applications.

Does LLM Alignment Really Need Diversity? An Empirical Study of Adapting RLVR Methods for Moral Reasoning
arXiv:2603.10588 | cs.AI

Problem

Whether LLM alignment tasks inherently require diversity-seeking algorithms rather than reward-maximizing methods remains unclear.

Method

Comprehensive empirical study on MoReBench comparing distribution-matching and reward-maximizing approaches using rubric-grounded reward pipeline.

Results

Contrary to hypothesis, distribution-matching shows no significant advantage over reward-maximizing methods; moral reasoning exhibits more concentrated high-reward distributions than mathematical reasoning.

Conclusion

Alignment tasks do not inherently require diversity-preserving algorithms; standard RLVR methods can effectively transfer to moral reasoning.

Meta-Evaluation of Vision-Language Models as Auditors of Autonomous Computer-Use Agents
arXiv:2603.10577 | cs.AI

Problem

Evaluating Computer-Use Agents (CUAs) reliably is challenging as static benchmarks and rule-based checks are brittle and poorly aligned with real-world usage.

Method

Large-scale meta-evaluation of five VLMs as autonomous auditors across three CUA benchmarks on macOS, Windows, and Linux, analyzing accuracy, calibration, and inter-model agreement.

Results

State-of-the-art VLMs achieve strong accuracy but show performance degradation in complex environments and significant disagreement in judgments.

Conclusion

Current model-based auditing approaches have fundamental limitations; explicit accounting for evaluator reliability, uncertainty, and variance is needed for real-world CUA deployment.

A Training Dataset to Improve Instruction Hierarchy on Frontier LLMs
arXiv:2603.10521 | cs.AI

Problem

Robust instruction hierarchy (IH) behavior is difficult to train due to confounding with instruction-following failures, nuanced conflicts, and shortcut learning like overrefusing.

Method

IH-Challenge dataset with reinforcement learning training and online adversarial example generation using Qwen3-1.7B judge model for rubric-grounded rewards.

Results

Fine-tuning GPT-5-Mini improves IH robustness by +10.0% across 16 benchmarks, reduces unsafe behavior from 6.6% to 0.7%, and saturates agentic prompt injection evaluation.

Conclusion

Structured adversarial training datasets with verifiable rewards significantly improve instruction hierarchy robustness without capability regression.
