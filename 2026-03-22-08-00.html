A Scalable Critic Framework for Generalist GUI Rewards GUI Agents

Zehao Li, Zhenyu Wu, Yibo Zhao, et al.
Problem: RL training for GUI agents is sensitive to reward quality, and existing approaches struggle with scalability and performance.

Method: OS-Themis - a multi-agent critic framework that decomposes trajectories into verifiable milestones and employs a review mechanism to audit evidence chains.

Results: 10.3% improvement in online RL training on AndroidWorld; 6.9% gain in trajectory validation.

Conclusion: Decomposed multi-agent criticism with evidence auditing drives agent evolution more effectively than single-judge approaches.

A Process-Control Architecture for Reliable LLM Reasoning LLM Safety

Qiang Zou et al.
Problem: LLMs remain vulnerable to hallucination and unreliable reasoning under adversarial prompting despite RLHF and output filtering.

Method: Box Maze framework - three-layer architecture with memory grounding, structured inference, and boundary enforcement across DeepSeek-V3, Doubao, and Qwen.

Results: Boundary failure rates reduced from ~40% (baseline RLHF) to below 1% under adversarial conditions in 50 scenarios.

Conclusion: Explicit cognitive control layers at the process level may offer a promising direction for improving LLM reliability.

cuGenOpt: GPU-Accelerated General-Purpose Metaheuristic Framework Optimization

Yuyang Liu et al.
Problem: Combinatorial optimization faces trade-offs among generality, performance, and usability.

Method: CUDA-based framework with "one block evolves one solution" architecture, unified encoding abstraction, adaptive operator selection, and JIT-compiled Python API with LLM assistant.

Results: Outperforms MIP solvers by orders of magnitude; achieves 4.73% gap on TSP-442 within 30s; 75-81% VRPTW throughput improvement.

Conclusion: GPU-accelerated metaheuristics with unified abstractions can match specialized solvers while maintaining generality.

D5P4: Partition Determinantal Point Process for Diversity in Parallel Discrete Diffusion Decoding Diffusion

Jonathan Lys et al.
Problem: Discrete diffusion models lack effective decoding methods with controllable in-batch diversity.

Method: Generalized beam-search framework with D5P4 instantiation that formulates selection as MAP inference over Determinantal Point Processes using scalable greedy solver.

Results: Improved diversity over strong baselines while maintaining competitive generation quality on free-form generation and question answering.

Conclusion: Determinantal Point Processes enable explicit probability-diversity trade-offs with near-zero compute overhead in diffusion decoding.

Implicit Patterns in LLM-Based Binary Analysis Security

Qiang Li et al.
Problem: How multi-pass LLM-based vulnerability analysis organizes exploration over hundreds of reasoning steps is poorly understood.

Method: Large-scale trace-level study analyzing 521 binaries with 99,563 reasoning steps to identify token-level implicit patterns.

Results: Four dominant patterns identified: early pruning, path-dependent lock-in, targeted backtracking, and knowledge-guided prioritization.

Conclusion: LLM reasoning in security analysis exhibits stable, structured implicit patterns that form a foundation for more reliable analysis systems.

How Uncertainty Estimation Scales with Sampling in Reasoning Models Uncertainty

Maksym Del et al.
Problem: Uncertainty estimation for reasoning language models under extended chain-of-thought remains poorly understood.

Method: Study of parallel sampling as black-box approach using verbalized confidence and self-consistency across 3 reasoning models and 17 tasks.

Results: Hybrid estimator with just 2 samples improves AUROC by up to +12 points on average; mathematics shows stronger complementarity and faster scaling than STEM/humanities.

Conclusion: Signal combination is more effective than scaling single signals; domain-dependent effects suggest RLVR post-training influences uncertainty quality.

LuMamba: Latent Unified Mamba for Electrode Topology-Invariant EEG Modeling Bio AI

Thorir Mar Ingolfsson et al.
Problem: EEG foundation models face challenges from differing electrode topologies and computational scalability of Transformers.

Method: LuMamba combines LUNA's learned-query cross-attention for channel unification with FEMBA's bidirectional Mamba blocks and LeJEPA self-supervised learning.

Results: 80.99% balanced accuracy on TUAB; state-of-art on Alzheimer's detection (0.97 AUPR); 377× fewer FLOPs; scales to 12× longer sequences.

Conclusion: State-space models with learned latent representations enable efficient, topology-invariant EEG foundation models with only 4.6M parameters.

Serendipity by Design: Evaluating Cross-domain Mappings on Human and LLM Creativity Creativity

Qiawen Liu et al.
Problem: Whether LLMs are creative like humans and respond to same interventions is unclear.

Method: Comparative study of humans and LLMs generating product features under cross-domain mapping (random source) vs. user-need prompts.

Results: Humans benefit reliably from cross-domain mappings; LLMs generate more original ideas on average but show no significant effect; impact increases with semantic distance in both.

Conclusion: Remote associations enhance creativity, but humans and LLMs respond differently to the same interventions, highlighting systematic differences in creative processes.

Artificial Intelligence and Judicial Decision Making AI Ethics

Arthur Dyevre et al.
Problem: AI integration into judicial decision-making raises concerns about transparency, reliability, and accountability.

Method: Synthetic review connecting AI tool performance/fairness, human judge biases, and AI+human interactions across computer science, economics, law, criminology, and psychology.

Results: Existing evidence shows modest or no impact of AI decision aids on pretrial and sentencing decisions; important gaps remain in understanding judge responses to AI advice.

Conclusion: AI vs. Human comparisons can yield insights into both algorithmic tools and human decision-makers; greater interdisciplinary integration is needed.

Behavioral Fingerprints for LLM Endpoint Stability and Identity MLOps

Jonah Leshin et al.
Problem: Traditional reliability metrics don't capture behavioral changes when model endpoints change due to updates, quantization, or infrastructure changes.

Method: Stability Monitor - black-box system that fingerprints endpoints by sampling outputs from fixed prompts and comparing distributions over time using energy distance statistics.

Results: Detects changes to model family, version, inference stack, quantization, and behavioral parameters; reveals substantial provider-to-provider and within-provider stability differences.

Conclusion: Output distribution fingerprinting enables detection of silent behavioral changes that traditional monitoring misses.
